## 1. The Roofline: The Ultimate Constraint

Training billion-parameter models hits a fundamental limit: you're either **compute bound** (limited by how fast the math runs) or **communication bound** (limited by data movement between memory and across chips). The roofline defines where modern training runs hit the ceiling.

## 2. GPU Architecture: Modular, Tensor-Core-Centric

Modern AI GPUs (NVIDIA H100, B200) are built as massive arrays of **streaming multiprocessors** (SMs), each with specialized **tensor cores** for matrix multiplication. They are optimized for one primary task.

## 4. GPU vs TPU: Same Philosophy, Different Paths

Both NVIDIA GPUs and Google TPUs center on dedicated matrix-multiplication units (Tensor Cores vs MXU). The difference is implementation and ecosystem, not core design philosophy.

## 🎯 Actionable Insights for AI Investors

<Callout variant="insight" title="✨ Top takeaways">
1. **Roofline matters at scale** – Communication bottlenecks (memory, networking) often limit training before raw compute. Invest in companies addressing interconnect and memory bandwidth, not only peak FLOPS.
2. **Tensor-core roadmaps are key** – Vendors with sustained tensor-core doubling (V100 → B200) are structurally aligned with LLM workloads.
3. **GPU vs TPU** – Both pursue the same core idea. The competition is in ecosystem, deployment, and total cost of ownership.
</Callout>
